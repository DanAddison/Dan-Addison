Portfolio

When I'm not building bespoke WordPress sites with the team at Nebulo, I'm working on solo freelance projects. Here are some examples:

JM Woodcraft

Live site

A commercial website for a carpenter.

Jack wanted a shop-window for his woodcrafting and carpentry skills. His key objective was to be able to demonstrate to visitors the breadth of his services, and that he has an active, thriving business with a healthy turnover of projects.

We identified that the WordPress content management system would provide the platform he needed to show this. I created a simple, minimal theme with a 'project' custom post type. This enables him to easily upload images and info about his handiwork over time via a user-friendly admin interface. These 'project' posts form an organically growing archive of jobs which are sorted under categories and tags, and thus easily navigated by a visitor looking for evidence of a particular type of work. Images added to a post automatically display in a carousel slider, so users are served a very detailed visual document of Jack's work.

Tech Stack:
WordPress, Sass, jQuery, Gulp, Git

Pen & Plant

Live site

An online portfolio and shop for an illustrator.

The project was conceived to provide a showcase for the client's self-directed collections of botanical illustrations, and attract commissions for such work. Pen & Plant had an existing brand identity as a designer/maker of small decorative objects and wearables, so the website also needed to incorporate e-commerce for selling these items directly to the customer.

I created a site that attempts to balance a neutral aesthetic, to highlight the client's own work, with the pragmatic functionality neccessary to make it an effective, accessible, fully-featured online shop.

I chose to build a bespoke child-theme of the Storefront theme, made by WooCommerce themselves, so it is a well supported, lightweight basis to build upon. I have used minimal 3rd party plugins to keep things maintainable, secure, bloat-free and performant.

Tech Stack:
WordPress, WooCommerce, Sass, jQuery, Gulp, Git

John Hosking

Live site

A portfolio website for an artist.

John's family wanted to celebrate John's life and work with a website to catalogue decades of painting and printmaking.

As this would become a fixed archive, as opposed to a dynamic one with content being added or changed over time, we decided to build a static site. With no content management system to provide a user-friendly interface for the family to add their exhaustive archive of work themselves to the finished site, I took on the task of populating the site with all of its content.

We chose this approach as there are clear benefits to building a static site. The site itself is faster as there is no server-side dynamic rendering of resources. It is very secure as there is no database or CMS to breach. Hosting costs are extremely low, as the site requires minimal server resources. This means that the family avoid ongoing maintenance tasks and expenses in keeping John's fast, responsive portfolio site up and running.

Tech Stack:
Sass, Nunjucks, jQuery, Gulp, Git
